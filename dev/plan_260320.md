# Research Plan: Testing the Nonadiabatic Framework Post-NMR

## Status

Phase 0 for NMR $R_{1\rho}$ returned **NEGATIVE** on March 20, 2026. The nonadiabatic correction is $6.3 \times 10^{-11}$ relative — eleven orders of magnitude below the measurement threshold. The NMR experimental path is closed.

The framework, codebase, and theoretical foundations remain sound. The negative result establishes a quantitative criterion for viable test systems: the perturbation ratio $V/\Delta E$ must be $\gtrsim 10^{-2}$ for the nonadiabatic correction to be measurable. NMR has $V/\Delta E \sim 10^{-5}$. We need systems with $V/\Delta E \sim 10^{-1}$.

## What We Have

### Theory
- Nonadiabatic Nakajima-Zwanzig framework (NNZ) with time-dependent projection operator
- BQP complexity constraint explaining why no classical master equation is exact
- Approximation hierarchy: exact NNZ → Born-Markov → nonadiabatic Redfield → nonadiabatic CGME → nonadiabatic secular Lindblad
- Technical gaps document identifying open issues (projection operator definition, complete positivity of exact NNZ, detailed balance, near-degeneracy)
- Phase 0 lab report with the NMR negative result

### Codebase (200+ tests, all passing)
- `SpinSystem`: NMR spin-1/2 systems with Zeeman + J-coupling Hamiltonians
- `SpinLockDrive`: rotating-frame spin-lock with analytical derivatives
- `SpectralDensityBath`: microscopic Redfield tensor from spectral density, with full Redfield, secular Lindblad, and CGME dissipators
- `NonadiabaticDecomposition`: Landau-Lifshitz integration by parts, plateau invariance verified
- `Simulator`: RK4 propagation with eigenbasis transformation, three dissipator methods
- `R1rhoExtractor`: exponential fitting of observable decay
- `nmr_analytical`: Solomon-Bloembergen benchmark rates
- `nonadiabatic_estimate`: Phase 0 feasibility machinery

### Key Quantitative Result
The correction scales as $(V/\Delta E)^2$. The table from the lab report:

| System | $V/\Delta E$ | Expected correction |
|:---|:---|:---|
| NMR $R_{1\rho}$ | $\sim 10^{-5}$ | $\sim 10^{-10}$ (unmeasurable) |

## Plan

### Phase 0A: HITRAN Line Mixing Feasibility Estimate

**Goal:** Determine whether the nonadiabatic correction to pressure-broadened rotational line mixing is large enough to be measurable against HITRAN data.

**Why HITRAN is promising:**
- The relaxation matrix for collisional line mixing IS the Redfield tensor — same math, different system
- Perturbation ratios $V/\Delta E \sim 0.1$ for adjacent rotational lines at moderate pressures
- The HITRAN database contains measured line-mixing parameters with published uncertainties
- Standard Redfield theory is already the workhorse for line-mixing calculations; known discrepancies with experiment exist
- The data is public and free

**What to compute:**

1. **System Hamiltonian.** A rigid rotor with rotational constant $B$ (e.g., CO with $B \approx 1.93$ cm$^{-1}$). Energy levels $E_J = BJ(J+1)$, Bohr frequencies $\omega_{J,J'} = B[J(J+1) - J'(J'+1)]$. For adjacent lines in the Q-branch, $\Delta E \sim 2B \approx 4$ cm$^{-1}$ $\approx 7.5 \times 10^{11}$ rad/s.

2. **Perturbation (collisional coupling).** The off-diagonal elements of the relaxation matrix, which couple different rotational transitions through collisions with the buffer gas. These are of order $\gamma_{\text{pressure}} \sim 0.05$–$0.1$ cm$^{-1}$/atm $\approx 10^{10}$ rad/s at 1 atm. This gives $V/\Delta E \sim 0.01$–$0.1$.

3. **Adiabatic mixing fraction.** $|a_k|^2 \sim (V/\Delta E)^2 \sim 10^{-4}$–$10^{-2}$.

4. **CGME sinc factors.** For adjacent rotational lines with $\Delta\omega \sim 10^{11}$ rad/s and a coarse-graining timescale $\Delta\tau$ set by the collision duration ($\sim 10^{-12}$ s), the sinc argument is $\Delta\omega \cdot \Delta\tau / 2 \sim 50$, giving sinc $\ll 1$ — similar to NMR. But for closely spaced lines in a dense Q-branch, $\Delta\omega$ can be much smaller, making the sinc factors $O(1)$ and the CGME genuinely different from secular Lindblad.

5. **Comparison against HITRAN.** If the correction is $\gtrsim 1\%$, compare the standard Redfield line-mixing prediction against the nonadiabatic Redfield prediction for a specific molecular system (CO or CO$_2$ Q-branch), using the same published intermolecular potential, and check which matches the HITRAN data better.

**Estimated effort:** 1–2 weeks for the feasibility estimate; 2–4 additional weeks for the full comparison if positive.

**Codebase changes needed:**
- `RotationalSystem` class (replaces `SpinSystem`): rigid rotor energy levels, transition dipole matrix elements, selection rules
- Collisional spectral density model (replaces `LipariSzabo`): pressure-broadening cross-sections from published intermolecular potentials
- Adapt `nonadiabatic_estimate.py` to compute the correction for rotational line mixing
- Everything else (Redfield tensor, CGME, NonadiabaticDecomposition, Simulator) reuses unchanged

**Go/no-go:** If the estimated correction is $< 10^{-3}$ relative, HITRAN is also eliminated and we move to Phase 0B.

### Phase 0B: FTMW Coherent Transients Feasibility Estimate

**Goal:** Determine whether the nonadiabatic correction to coherent free induction decay in Fourier-transform microwave spectroscopy is measurable.

**Why FTMW is promising:**
- Chirped microwave pulses sweep across rotational transitions with well-characterized time-frequency profiles
- The pulse is the perturbation, and the molecular beam provides (weak) collisional dissipation
- The perturbation ratio depends on the pulse power and sweep rate — it can be tuned
- Brooks Pate's group publishes complete pulse specifications and molecular parameters

**What to compute:**
- For a molecule with nuclear quadrupole hyperfine structure (closely spaced transitions), compute $|b_k(t)|^2$ and $|c_k(t)|^2$ during and after a chirped pulse
- Estimate the difference in the FID envelope predicted by the two frameworks
- Determine whether the difference exceeds the signal-to-noise ratio of published CP-FTMW data

**Estimated effort:** 2–3 weeks.

**Codebase changes needed:**
- `ChirpedPulse` signal class (frequency sweeps linearly with time)
- Rotational Hamiltonian with hyperfine structure
- Everything else reuses unchanged

**Go/no-go:** If the predicted FID difference is below the noise floor, FTMW is also eliminated.

### Phase 0C: HCl Vibration-Rotation (Hunt's Own System)

**Goal:** If HITRAN and FTMW both fail, go directly to the system where Hunt has already computed 35% differences: HCl vibration-rotation transitions under pulsed infrared excitation.

**Why this is the fallback:**
- Mandal and Hunt (2018) already showed that $|b_k(t)|^2$ and $|c_k(t)|^2$ differ by up to 35% for overlapping IR pulses
- The molecular parameters are known to extraordinary precision from HITRAN
- The perturbation ratio for IR excitation of HCl can be $V/\Delta E \sim 0.01$–$0.1$

**The challenge:**
- Finding published time-resolved population measurements with fully characterized pulse shapes
- IR spectroscopy of gas-phase HCl is mostly frequency-domain (line positions and intensities), not time-domain (population dynamics during a pulse)
- May need to work with Hunt directly to identify the right experimental data or propose a new experiment

**Estimated effort:** Variable — depends on data availability.

**Paper structure:**

3. The nonadiabatic CGME as a concrete, implementable approximation
4. The NMR $R_{1\rho}$ Phase 0 result (honest negative, with the $V/\Delta E$ criterion)
5. The experimental search landscape (which systems have the right perturbation ratio)

**Target journals:** J. Chem. Phys. (where the Mandal-Hunt papers live) or Phys. Rev. A (open quantum systems)

**Co-authorship:** Discuss with Hunt.
